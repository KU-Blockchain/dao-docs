# Understanding Tokens

Imagine going to an arcade and trading $10 for arcade tokens. Those tokens aren't worth anything in the real world, but inside the arcade, you can use them to play games and keep score.

**Digital tokens work the same way.**

A digital token is digital money that lives on the blockchain. In a DAO, you get tokens when you complete tasks or contribute. You can then hold those tokens, trade them, or use them to vote on decisions on the DAO.

## Two Types of Tokens

**Regular Tokens (like money)**
- These are all the same. One token is worth exactly the same as another token.
- Example: Dollar bills. A $1 bill is worth the same as any other $1 bill.
- Used for: Payments, rewards, voting power.

**Special Tokens (NFTs)**
- These are unique. Each one is different and one-of-a-kind.
- Example: A football match ticket with a specific seat number.
- Used for: Digital art, achievement badges, membership passes.

## How You Earn & Use Tokens in a DAO

1. **You do work for the DAO** (like writing docs or coding)
2. **The DAO sends you tokens** as payment
3. **You hold tokens in your wallet** (like a digital piggy bank)
4. **You use tokens to vote** on DAO decisions

## Why Tokens Are Cool

- **They can't be faked.** Because they're on the blockchain, everyone can see exactly how many exist and who owns them. No counterfeits.
- **Anyone can receive them.** You can send tokens to someone in another country instantly, no bank needed.
- **You get a say.** More tokens = more voting power in DAO decisions.
- **They have value.** You earned them, so they're yours to keep, sell, or trade.

## What Tokens Do in a DAO

- **Give you voting rights** - Your tokens = your vote on what happens next
- **Pay you for work** - Get tokens when you complete tasks  
- **Control who joins** - Some DAOs only let token holders access special channels or tools
